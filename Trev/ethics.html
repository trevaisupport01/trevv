Academic Ethics

Last updated: July 21, 2026

1. Responsible Use of AI

Our curriculum teaches powerful AI automation and generation tools. Students are expected to use these skills ethically and legally within their respective professions. You agree not to use the prompted workflows taught in this class to generate malicious code, engage in deepfake impersonation, or mass-produce spam.

2. Plagiarism and Disclosure

While we encourage the use of AI to enhance productivity, we advocate for transparency. When submitting capstone projects or utilizing AI for professional deliverables (such as reports, valuations, or content), you must adhere to the disclosure standards of your specific industry regarding AI-assisted work.

3. Fact-Checking and Hallucinations

AI models can produce inaccurate information (hallucinations). It is the student's ethical responsibility to verify, fact-check, and validate all AI-generated outputs before applying them to real-world business scenarios, client work, or academic submissions.

4. Community Conduct

Our private communities are safe spaces for collaborative learning. Any harassment, spamming, or theft of other students' shared prompts or ideas within the community will result in immediate removal from the group.
